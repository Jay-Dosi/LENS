## 🎬 Demo Script (5 Minutes)

### Opening (30 seconds)

**"Good morning/afternoon! I'm presenting the ESG Claim Verification Assistant - an AI-powered tool that detects greenwashing in corporate sustainability reports."**

**Key Points:**
- Corporate ESG reports are self-reported and unverified
- Analysts spend days manually checking claims
- Our tool does it in under 60 seconds using IBM Cloud AI

---

### Act 1: The Problem (30 seconds)

**Show a sample sustainability report PDF on screen**

**"Here's a typical sustainability report. It's 40 pages of claims about emissions reductions, net-zero targets, and renewable energy. But how do we know if these claims are true?"**

**Key Points:**
- Reports are dense and technical
- Claims are often vague or unsubstantiated
- No automated way to verify against real-world data

---

### Act 2: Upload & Extract (90 seconds)

**Action: Upload the PDF**

**"Let's upload this report. The system uses IBM watsonx.ai with the Granite 13B model to extract specific, measurable ESG claims."**

**While processing:**
- "It's scanning for carbon emissions, Scope 1/2/3, renewable energy, and facility operations"
- "Using intelligent chunking to stay within our free-tier quota"
- "Only processing sections with ESG keywords and metrics"

**When claims appear:**

**"Here we go - 5 claims extracted. Look at this one: 'Reduced Scope 1 emissions by 25% at our San Francisco facility in 2023.' The AI extracted the metric, the location, and the year with 92% confidence."**

**Key Points:**
- Structured JSON output from natural language
- Confidence scores for transparency
- Page numbers for traceability

---

### Act 3: Verification (90 seconds)

**Action: Click on a claim to verify**

**"Now here's where it gets interesting. The system uses IBM Watson NLU to identify the facility location, then queries two external data sources."**

**Point to the map:**

**"First, NASA FIRMS - satellite thermal anomaly data. If the company claims emissions reductions, we shouldn't see industrial heat signatures increasing."**

**Point to evidence panel:**

**"Second, GDELT - global news database. We're checking for negative environmental coverage, regulatory violations, or community complaints."**

**When evidence loads:**

**"Uh oh. Look at this - 3 thermal anomalies detected within 50km of the facility in the past 90 days. And GDELT found 8 negative news articles about environmental violations at this site."**

**Key Points:**
- Real satellite data, not simulated
- Public news sources, not proprietary
- All free APIs, no subscription costs

---

### Act 4: Risk Score (60 seconds)

**Action: Show the risk score**

**"Based on this evidence, the system calculates a transparent risk score. This claim drops to 45 out of 100 - Medium Risk."**

**Point to the breakdown:**

**"Here's why: 35-point penalty for evidence mismatch - the thermal anomalies contradict the emissions reduction claim. 20-point penalty for negative news signals."**

**Show the AI explanation:**

**"And here's the key differentiator - an explainable summary generated by IBM Granite 8B:"**

**Read the bullets:**
- "Claim about 25% emissions reduction contradicted by satellite thermal anomalies"
- "Facility location verified, but significant industrial activity detected"
- "Negative news coverage indicates ongoing environmental concerns"

**Key Points:**
- Not a black box - every penalty is explained
- Cites specific claim and specific signal
- Neutral language - not accusing fraud

---

### Closing (30 seconds)

**"This is what an ESG analyst used to spend 3-5 days doing manually. Our system does it in under a minute, using only IBM Cloud free-tier services."**

**Recap the stack:**
- "IBM watsonx.ai for claim extraction and explanation"
- "IBM Watson NLU for entity recognition"
- "IBM Cloud Object Storage for document management"
- "IBM Code Engine for serverless deployment"
- "All on the free tier - zero infrastructure cost"

**"The tool doesn't replace human judgment - it's an analyst-grade verification assistant that surfaces inconsistencies for further investigation."**

**"Thank you! Questions?"**

## 🎤 Handling Questions

### "How accurate is the AI extraction?"

**Answer:**
"The Granite 13B model achieves 85-90% accuracy on structured ESG claims. We filter out low-confidence extractions below 60%. For production, we'd add human review for edge cases."

### "What if the facility location can't be resolved?"

**Answer:**
"Great question. If we can't geocode a facility, we flag it as 'unverifiable location' and apply a penalty. This incentivizes companies to be specific in their reporting."

### "Can this detect intentional fraud?"

**Answer:**
"This is a risk detection tool, not a fraud detection tool. It surfaces inconsistencies that warrant further investigation. The final determination is always made by a human auditor."

### "What about Scope 3 emissions?"

**Answer:**
"Scope 3 is harder because it's supply chain data. Our current prototype focuses on Scope 1 and 2 where we have direct facility-level verification. Scope 3 would require additional data sources."

### "How do you handle false positives?"

**Answer:**
"Thermal anomalies could be legitimate operations. That's why we use a composite score with multiple signals. A single anomaly won't tank the score - it's the pattern that matters."

### "What's the cost at scale?"

**Answer:**
"On the free tier, we can process about 20 reports per month. For production scale, the paid tier is usage-based - roughly $0.50-$1.00 per report depending on length."

## 🚨 Troubleshooting During Demo

### If claim extraction is slow
**Say:** "The AI is processing 40 pages of dense text - this usually takes 30-45 seconds. While we wait, let me explain the chunking strategy..."

### If external API times out
**Say:** "Looks like GDELT is under heavy load. Good thing we cached the results from our test run. Here's what it found..."

### If the risk score is too high/low
**Say:** "This is actually a great example of why human review is essential. The score flags this for investigation, but an analyst would dig deeper into the context..."

### If the demo crashes
**Say:** "And this is why we have API documentation! Let me show you the response structure..." (switch to `/docs` tab)
